PROTECTION OF INFORMATION

Sears commitment to protecting personal customer information


Sears, Roebuck and Co. values the trust its customers place in the company.
Accordingly, Sears adheres to the highest ethical standards in gathering, using and safeguarding customer information that is entrusted to the company.
USE AND COLLECTION OF CUSTOMER INFORMATION


Sears does not rent, sell or exchange information about its customers.
Access to information about Sears customers is restricted to the Sears family of business, including all businesses that accept Sears payment vehicles.
Information regarding Sears credit accounts may also be shared with credit bureaus.
==========
Third parties that need access to Sears customer information in order to provide operational or other support services to Sears must agree to safeguard customer information in strict compliance with Sears policy.
Sears gathers only the customer information that is needed to administer its business, provide superior service and communicate offers on merchandise and services that Sears believes will be of interest to its customers.
Sears limits access to customer information to those employees who need it to carry out their business functions.
==========
Sears educates its employees about Sears policies and practices in regard to safeguarding customer information; preventing its unauthorized access, use or disclosure; and, ensuring its proper handling.
ACCURACY AND SECURITY OF CUSTOMER INFORMATION

Sears makes all reasonable efforts to ensure that the customer information it maintains is accurate, timely and secure.
Sears will monitor and adopt, as appropriate, new technological developments that are designed to aid in ensuring the accuracy and security of customer information.

ONLINE PRIVACY

Personally-Identifiable Information

Sears world wide web sites collect personally-identifiable information (such as name, address, telephone number, or email address) from web site visitors only when visitors voluntarily provide such information to Sears.
Sears web site visitors who do not want their personally-identifiable information collected online are asked not to submit such information to Sears.
Sears web site visitors are informed that when they provide Sears with personally-identifiable information online,  that information is kept within the Sears family of businesses.
Visitors are further informed that, unless specifically stated otherwise, the personally-identifiable information may be stored and used by Sears to better understand its customers' needs, to improve Sears products and services, and to contact customers regarding offers that may be of interest to them.
If Sears web site visitors previously provided their email addresses to Sears and would like to have their email addresses removed from Sears email mailing lists,  Sears asks them to send an email message to webmaster@ Sears.com.
Sears informs such visitors that it will use reasonable efforts to delete their email addresses from its existing files.
Children
Sears has no intention of collecting personally-identifiable information (name, address, telephone number, or email address) from children (individuals under the age of 13) online and using that information for marketing purposes.
==========
Where appropriate, Sears will specifically instruct children not to submit such information on Sears web sites.
If a child has provided Sears with an email address, Sears asks that a parent or guardian of the child contact Sears at webmaster@sears.com if they would like the email address deleted from Sears email mailing lists.
Sears informs customers that it will use reasonable efforts to delete such email addresses from its existing files.
==========
Non-Personally Identifiable Information Collected Automatically

Sears web sites may collect information from web site visitors that is not personally-identifiable,  including the visitor's operating system, browser, domain name, and the specific web pages on the Sears Web site that are accessed or visited.
Sears only collects this information in the aggregate, and only uses it to improve Sears web sites.
Information Placed Automatically on Visitors' Computers
Sears informs web site visitors that when they visit Sears web sites, Sears may store some information on the visitor's computer in the form of a "cookie" or similar file.
These files allow Sears to tailor a Website to reflect a customer's listed or historical preferences.
Most Web browsers allow Web users to exercise control over such files on their computers by erasing them, blocking them, or notifying the user when such a file is stored.
Sears web site visitors are asked to refer to their browser's instructions to learn about those functions.
