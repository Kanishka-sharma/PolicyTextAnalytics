Cookies

A cookie is a small text file containing a unique identification
number that is transferred from a website to the hard drive of your
computer allowing the website to identify you and track your
activities on our site.
Cookies enable us to track and target the
interests of our users to simplify and enhance the experience on our
site.
Once the user closes their browser, the cookie information is
no longer available through our server.
You can set your browser to
notify you when you receive a cookie, giving you the chance to decide
whether to accept it.
If a user rejects the cookie, they may still
use our site.
Log Files

We use IP addresses in log files to analyze trends, administer the
site, track a user's movements, and gather broad demographic
information for aggregate use.
Log files do not enable us to access
personally identifying information, such as email addresses.
