This Mobile Privacy Statement applies to our mobile and social media apps and mobile websites.
This  Mobile Privacy Statement (“Privacy Statement”) describes how Discover Financial  Services (including Discover Bank, Discover Products Inc., and their  affiliates) treat personal information on our mobile apps and websites, and  apps on social media platforms (“Mobile Services”).
This Privacy Statement only describes Mobile  Service practices.
==========
It does not apply to how we generally collect or use other  information we collect from or about you as a customer or applicant of any  Discover financial service or product.
For more information about privacy as it  applies to your financial account, please see the
Consumer Privacy Statementfor  the Discover product(s) you have.
It also doesn’t apply to the practices  on our websites not optimized for a mobile browser.
For more information  about privacy on our websites, please see our Discover.com Website Privacy Statement.
Our Mobile Services  collect information from and about you.
==========
Information you submit.
Our  Mobile Services may ask you to submit personal information like your name,  account number and account ID.
Our Mobile Services may also ask for your  email address or other contact information.
We may collect your zip code or  location to help you find an ATM or other information or offers you request.
Financial information.
Our Mobile Services may ask for bank  account information for funds transfers to complete a service.
They may  also ask for images of checks as part of a deposit.
How we use financial  information is described in our                              Consumer Privacy Statements.
Other information.
==========
If you use our Mobile Services, we may collect information about the browser,  device or operating system you’re using.
We may also collect your device ID and  advertising ID.
We might look at what site you came from, or what site you  visit when you leave us.
When you use our Mobile Services, we may collect your  location using GPS, a cellular network location, wi-fi networks, browser  services, or information you provide.
Our Mobile Services  collect information in different ways.
Information is collected directly  from you.
We collect information if you request to receive offers  and alerts through a Mobile Service.
For  example, when you select an offer through the Mobile Services we will collect  information from that specific action.
Information is collected passively.
Our Mobile Services and some emails may use  tracking tools like browser cookies, pixel tags and web beacons.
==========
Learn more  about these tools, and how you can control them, here.
We get information about you from  third parties.
For example, our business partners  and service providers may give us information about you.
Social media sites may  also give us information about you.
We use information from our Mobile Services in many ways.
==========
We use information to respond to  your requests or questions.
For example, we might use your  information to complete a transaction that you requested.
We may also use  your information to redeem your rewards or send you an alert you signed up for.
==========
We use information to improve our  products and services.
We may use your information to  make our Mobile Services better or to customize your experience with us.
We may  combine information we get from you with information about you we get from  third parties.
We use information for security  purposes.
We may use information, including your location  information, to protect our company or our customers.
For example, if you have  location services turned on, we might use your mobile location information to  help identify potential fraudulent transactions.
We might do this by ensuring transactions on  your card occur from the same location where your phone is located.
==========
We use information for marketing purposes.
For example, we may use information we know  about you from our Mobile Services, including your location information, to send  you information about new products and special offers.
These may come from us  or our partners.
We might tell you about new features or updates we think you  will like.
We may also use push notifications on our mobile apps.
==========
Learn how to manage your choices for marketing communications.
We  use passive technologies for analytical and marketing purposes.
Discover  and its service providers may gather information about you over time across  multiple websites, mobile apps or other platforms.
We may use passive  technologies such as cookies, web beacons, pixel tags, location information and  device-level advertising and user identifiers.
==========
We use device IDs to uniquely identify your device.
We also use them to  identify the screens you view within our Mobile Services from time to time.
We  do this to improve functionality of our Mobile Services.
It also allows us to send targeted messages  and offers to you.
==========
We (or our service providers on our behalf) may use passive  technologies like advertising IDs to post advertising content on third-party  mobile apps or websites.
We may use web beacons and pixel tags to provide information  to us and to third parties about sites you visit after seeing Discover ads or  offers in mobile browsers.
Learn how you  can control tracking tools.
==========
We use information to communicate  with you about your account or our relationship.
We may contact you about your account or feedback.
We might also contact you  about this Privacy Statement or our Terms of Use.
We use information as otherwise  permitted by law.
We keep personal information  as long as it is necessary or relevant for the practices described in this  Privacy Statement.
We also keep information as otherwise required by law.

We will share this information  within the Discover Financial Services family of companies.
For  example, this includes Discover Bank and Discover Home Loans, Inc.

==========
We will share this information with our  service providers.
For example, we share personal information  such as your phone number or email address with vendors who send texts or  emails on our behalf.
We may share information with companies who perform  advertising services on our behalf.
We may also share information with  companies that operate or analyze our Mobile Services.

We will share this information with  our business partners.
For example, we may share  information with social media sites.

You have certain choices about our Mobile  Service sharing and marketing practices.
==========
You can control cookies and tracking  tools.
Your mobile browser may give you the ability to  control cookies.
How you do so depends on the type of cookie.
==========
Certain browsers can be set to clear past and reject future cookies.
If  you block cookies on your browser, certain features on our mobile website may  not work.
Additionally, if you block or delete cookies, not  all of the tracking activities we have described here will stop.
Choices you  make are both browser and device-specific.
You can control tools on your mobile  devices.
For example, you can turn off the  GPS locator or push notifications on your phone.
If your phone allows,  you can change app-specific location settings.
==========
You can opt out of receiving our marketing  communications.
For        existing customers, you have the ability to control your marketing        preferences by logging in to your Discover account and updating your        profile page.
To stop receiving our        promotional emails or texts, you can follow the instructions in any        promotional message you receive from Discover.
Even if you opt out        of getting marketing messages, we may still send you transactional        messages, including responses to your questions or information about your        account.
==========
To control targeted        advertising you receive on third-party mobile apps or websites, you can control        the advertising privacy settings on your device (for example, “Limit Ad        Tracking” on iOS devices or “Opt out of interest-based ads” on Android        devices).
If you receive targeted        ads in mobile browsers that accept cookies, you can also click on the        AdChoices opt-out logo in the corner of the advertisement or visit        AboutAds.info.
This opt-out is browser- and device-specific.
If you delete cookies after you opt        out, you will need to opt out again on AdChoices because the opt-out is        stored as a cookie.
Please note        that opt-outs may take up to 48 hours to process and you may still        continue to see our generic advertising even if you opt out of targeted        ads.
