Data collected via the Website
Our Website is designed primarily for our Clients and prospective Clients to better understand the Service, and to register for the Service.
==========
The information we obtain via the Website includes:
1.
User-Provided Information: When a User registers to utilize the Service via the Website, we will ask them to provide personally identifiable information such as a first name, last name, and email address.
Users may also choose to provide personally identifiable information to Sift Science such as mailing addresses or phone number, when they send an email to Sift Science, when they update the information previously provided to Sift Science, or at other times when they or access the Website.
2.
==========
Automatically Collected Information: When Users access the Website, Sift Science will automatically record certain information about or related to their use of the Website that is made available through their computer or other device such as an IP address, information about their computer’s operating system, pages visited on the Website, information that may enable Sift Science to approximate the User’s location, web pages or sites that visited just before or just after visiting the Website, the pages or screens accessed via the Website, the dates and times Users access the Website, and other information about how the Website is utilized.
This information is collected through the use of technologies such as standard log files, cookies, and web beacons.
3.
==========
Cookies Information: One form of “automatically collected” information we may record is information stored by “cookies” which small text files placed onto your computer or device.
When Users access the Website, Sift Science and our third-party analytics and site optimization partners may send one or more cookies to their respective computers or other devices.
By using cookies with values that are unique, Sift Science may be able to uniquely identify a computer or device when a User visits the Website for analytics purposes or to help our Website function better.
Sift Science may use both “session” and “persistent” cookies to collect, store, and sometimes track various types of information.
A session cookie is one that is intended to disappear after a User closes their browser.
A persistent cookie remains after a User closes his or her browser.
For more information about cookies and web beacons, please visit http://www.allaboutcookies.org/.
4.
==========
Web Beacons: A web beacon is a clear picture file used to keep track of a User’s navigation through a single website or a series of websites.
Web beacons, which are sometimes referred to as “clear gifs,” are a fancy way of referring to web links that a computer, browser, device, or email program requests when loading a web page, html email, or similar web-connected content.
When this request is made, the entity which controls the requested link (which would be Sift Science for links back to the Website) can determine if a User opened an email containing a web beacon or otherwise viewed particular content on the Website.
Sift Science generally uses web beacons for analytics and other legitimate business purposes.
