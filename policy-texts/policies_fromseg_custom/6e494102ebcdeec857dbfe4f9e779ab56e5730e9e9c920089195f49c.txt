How We Use The Information That We Gather 
 
AM uses certain technologies, such as "cookies," to improve your experience.
Cookies are alphanumeric identifiers that we transfer to your computer's hard drive.
Cookies store information about your visit to the AM Services or Sites and allow you to access AM's features.
The information in the cookie is transmitted from your computer to AM or one of our third-party service companies.
The information contained in these cookies makes it possible for our systems to know if you have logged into the Services or Sites during this visit or during a prior visit, allowing you to navigate the Services or Sites without having to repeatedly log in.
Cookies from AM and/or one of our service providers are also used to collect standard information transmitted from your web browser including: your Internet address, browser type, pages you visited with date and time, purchases you have made on the AM site and the operating system type on your computer.
This information is used to help us understand the needs of our subscribers.
==========
AM may also use cookies to track access into the message boards and forums areas of our Services or Sites and to track whether you have already completed one of our online surveys.
You always have the choice to set your browser to either warn you when a cookie is being accessed on your computer or to refuse to accept cookies.
However, to take full advantage of the features on AM's Services or Sites, we suggest you set your browser to accept cookies.
Setting your computer to accept or refuse cookies varies depending on the software you are running.
Please check the help tab of your browser for specifics on your software.
==========
AM also uses web beacon technology to help us track site usage.
Web beacons (also called single pixel gifs or action tags) allow us to track page viewing counts and patterns which we use to focus future content development, measure effectiveness of our own marketing campaigns and assist with capacity planning for the site.
AM also uses web beacons in certain e-mails we send to judge the effectiveness of these mailings.
These web beacons 'sense' if you open the e-mail, if your e-mail program is capable of displaying HTML messages and where you navigate within these HTML messages.
