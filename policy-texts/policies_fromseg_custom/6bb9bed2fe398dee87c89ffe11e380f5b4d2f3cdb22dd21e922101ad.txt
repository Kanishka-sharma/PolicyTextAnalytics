Privacy
Visitor Information
If you browse through an American Physical Society (APS) website, read pages, or download information, certain anonymous information about your visit will automatically be gathered and stored so that APS can continually evaluate and improve its visitors’ browsing experience.
Collected information does not identify you personally.
==========
The following information about your visit may be gathered and stored:
 


Internet domain or the IP address (e.g., college.edu) from which you access an APS website

Browser and operating system you used to access the site

Date and time you visited the APS website

Which pages you visited on the APS website

If you came to the APS website from another website, the address of that website.
Security
APS Computer Systems
APS websites reside on APS computer systems.
Unauthorized attempts to modify any information stored on APS systems, to impair or circumvent security features, or to use APS systems for other than their intended purposes, are illegal and may result in disciplinary action, criminal prosecution, or both.

Personal Information
We do not collect personal information about individuals except when specifically and knowingly provided by such individuals, such as during event registration.
You must be 13 years old or older to submit information to an APS website.
If you send us your email, address or personal information when you fill out a web page comment form, that information is only used to respond to your message.
Personal information collected through registration or applications is secure and maintained according to current industry standards.
Any personal information that you share with APS is safe-guarded so that unauthorized parties are prevented from accessing or viewing your information.
Only authorized APS system users have access to your information.

In transactions with APS, while APS will do everything within its power to keep your personal information secure, you acknowledge that there are security and privacy limitations of the Internet which are beyond our control.
Accessibility
APS is committed to providing accessible web pages for individuals with disabilities.
If you use assistive technology (such as a Braille reader, a screen reader, TTY, etc.) and the format of any material on an APS website is not accessible to you, please contact the APS Webmaster.
Email us the nature of your challenge, your preferred format for material, the web page URL, and your contact information, and we will try to provide you with that information, unless doing so places an undue burden on APS.

Linking Policy
Links on APS Websites
APS encourages you to exercise discretion while browsing the Internet.
Many APS web pages link to other websites.
We provide these links as a service but these websites are not controlled by APS and we cannot be responsible for their changing content.
Once another website is accessed from a link on APS, the copyright and licensing restrictions of the new site apply.
APS does not allow hyperlinks to any websites that advocate hate, bias or discrimination, or which contain information associated with partisan political activity.
Please notify us if you think we have inadvertently linked to a website containing unauthorized content.
==========
Linking to an APS Web Page
You may freely link to any public APS web page from personal or organizational websites.
Please link to APS rather than downloading portions of our content and pasting it on your site, so that our viewers will see our most up-to-date information.
The exception to this request is APS press releases and the APS Physics Jobs buttons.
The only credit you need is the website name and the title of the target web page.
Example: APS Physics Professional Development Guide.
