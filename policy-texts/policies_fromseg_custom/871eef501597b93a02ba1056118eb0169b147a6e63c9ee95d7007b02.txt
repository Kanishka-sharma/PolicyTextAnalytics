PRIVACY POLICY FOR THE NEW YORK TIMES
Last Updated on August 15, 2005
This policy discloses the privacy practices for The New York Times.
(The New York Times on the Web, which is a different division of The New York Times Company, maintains a separate privacy statement.)
The New York Times recognizes the privacy concerns of our customers and the importance of protecting the privacy of personally identifiable information collected from them.
We have therefore adopted a set of information management guidelines that safeguard your personally identifiable information.
==========
However, guidelines are subject to change.
Any such changes will be posted on this page.
If the changes represent a material departure from our current practices with respect to the use of personal information, the changes will be posted on this page thirty days prior to taking effect and Home Delivery subscribers who have registered to our Web site, will be notified via e-mail.
==========

What information does The New York Times gather about you?
==========
Registration for the Home Delivery Web site: During the user registration process on this site, we require that you submit your name, e-mail address (which is your unique User ID) and password.
We also require that you provide the answers to secret questions to be used when resetting your password.
This password will enable you to access New York Times Customer Care and New York Times on the Web (which includes TimesSelect content).
==========
Newspaper Subscriber information: We generally obtain your name, address, telephone number, credit card number or other billing information in order to process your subscription request.
As a subscriber, we request, that you also provide us with your e-mail address.
User accounts for the Home Delivery Web site: You may review and update your account information and access your transactions history.
Sweepstakes, Contests, Surveys, Special Offers: On occasion, The New York Times may collect additional personal information from you in connection with optional sweepstakes, contests, surveys or special offers.
If this information is to be shared with a third party, we will notify you at the time of collection.
==========
Online Reader Panel: Readers who are panel members agree to participate in periodic surveys about their readership of the Times, their household/personal characteristics and their purchase behavior.
Readers are primarily contacted via e-mail, which link to online surveys, but may occasionally be contacted by telephone or mail.
All survey data is reported/used in aggregate form only, and no personally identifiable information is released.
As a panel member, we require that you supply us with your e-mail address only; name, home address and telephone number are optional.
==========
Times Talks, Arts & Leisure Weekends, other NY Times Special Events and Promotions: We often receive information regarding subscribers who may also be customers of Times Talks, Arts & Leisure Weekends and other NY Times Special Events and Promotions.
Such information will be used as indicated at the time of collection.
New York Times Electronic Edition: The Electronic Edition is licensed by Newstand TM which has its own privacy statement.
Advertisers of The New York Times: For more privacy information please click here.
NYT Store: Click here to view privacy policy for the NYT Store.
==========
Cookies: The Home Delivery Web site employs cookies to recognize you and your access privileges on the site as well as to track site usage.
IP Addresses: This site logs IP addresses or the location of your computer on the Internet for systems administration and troubleshooting purposes.
This information is logged on a separate server from any personally identifiable information; our site's architecture does not allow IP information to be linked to any personal information, such as a user ID.
What does The New York Times do with the information it collects?
==========
We respect your privacy.
The personal information you provide us will be used to help make our future marketing efforts more efficient and to bring you offers of interest.
If at any time you wish to discontinue receiving any offers, you may opt-out by following the directions in the Opt Out section below.
We reserve the right to contact you to discuss your account or to renew your subscription, in accordance with applicable laws.
We also reserve the right to disclose your opt-out information to third parties so that they can "suppress" your name from future solicitations, in accordance with applicable laws.
With whom does The New York Times share the information it gathers?
From time to time, we provide trusted and responsible third parties with customer information, provided that such parties agree to use the information on a limited basis, and strictly in accordance with our standard usage and non-disclosure agreement.
Credit card, billing and financial information is never disclosed, except as needed to process the transaction, under strict confidentiality requirements.
==========
Statistical, Demographic and Marketing Analyses.
The New York Times may perform statistical, demographic and marketing analyses of subscribers and their subscribing and purchasing patterns for product development purposes and to generally inform advertisers about the nature of our subscriber base.
As a general rule, we view the data in aggregate form only.
We also use such information to allow advertising to be targeted, in aggregate, to the users for whom such advertising is the most pertinent.
Personal information about you as an individual will not be disclosed without your consent.
==========
E-mail and other communications.
We may periodically send you e-mails and other communications regarding your account.
Products and services from The New York Times.
From time to time, we may provide you with information and offers about other products and services offered by The New York Times, including subscriptions, online archives, and other premium products and services.
==========
This information will only be shared internally.
If you do not wish to receive such information or offers, you may opt-out.
Products and services from Third Parties or Partners.
From time to time, we may offer you the opportunity to receive information and offers about products or services from our partners or third parties.
==========
If you do not wish to receive such information or offers, you may opt-out.
How do I change or update my personal information?
You may update and/or edit your Home Delivery account online on the Home Delivery Web site by clicking on the link for Update Account .
==========
Should you require assistance, please call our toll free number 1-800-NYTIMES.
How do I opt out from receiving promotional offers?
Online Promotions: If, at any time, you prefer not to receive online marketing information from our marketing partners or us, simply follow the unsubscribe options at the bottom of the e-mail.
==========
Or click here to remove your e-mail address from any future e-mail campaigns originated by The New York Times Home Delivery Department, and its third party affiliates.
Offline Promotions: If, at any time, you prefer not to receive traditional mail or telephone solicitations originated by The New York Times Home Delivery Departments, and its third party affiliates, please e-mail us at 1-800@nytimes.com or write to us at: Customer Care, P.O. Box 217, Northvale, NJ 07647-0217.
Please remember to include your account number and phone number in the body of your e-mail or letter, and if you choose to opt out via e-mail, please put "Opt out" in the subject line.
Note: Even if you have opted out under this policy, if you register to The New York Times on the Web, you may be contacted via e-mail with offers from The New York Times on the Web, which is a different division of The New York Times Company.
Please visit www.nytimes.com/email
in order to update your e-mail preferences for The New York Times on the Web.
==========
Compliance with Legal Process
We may disclose personal information if we are required to do so by law or if we, in good faith, believe that such action is necessary to (1) comply with the law or with legal process; (2) protect and defend our rights and property; (3) protect against misuse or unauthorized use of this site, or of The New York Times; or to (4) protect the personal safety or property of our users or the public.
(For example, this means that if you provide false information or attempt to pose as someone else, information about you may be disclosed.)
Data Security
To prevent unauthorized access, maintain data security, and ensure the appropriate use of information, we have put in place appropriate physical, electronic, and managerial procedures to protect the information we collect online and offline.
