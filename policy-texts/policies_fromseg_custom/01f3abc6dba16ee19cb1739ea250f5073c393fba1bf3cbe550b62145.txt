PRIVACY POLICY
FOR THE NEW YORK TIMES HOME DELIVERY WEB SITE 
 
This policy discloses the privacy practices for The New York
Times, and for its Subscription and Customer Care Web Site.
The New York Times on the Web maintains a
separate privacy policy.
The New York Times recognizes the privacy concerns of our
subscribers and the importance of protecting the privacy of personally
identifiable information collected from them.
We have therefore adopted a set
of information management guidelines that safeguard your personally
identifiable information.
These
guidelines have been developed with the recognition that Internet technologies
are rapidly evolving and that underlying business models are still not
established.
Accordingly, guidelines
are subject to change.
Any such changes
will be posted on this page.
If the
changes represent a material departure from our current practices with respect
to the use of personal information, the changes will be posted on this page
thirty days prior to taking effect and registered users will be notified via
e-mail.
The New York Times Home Delivery Web Site is not responsible
for the content or the privacy policies of other Web Sites to which it may
link.
What information does The New York Times and this Home
Delivery Web Site gather about you?
==========
Online Registration: During
the user registration process on this site, we require that you submit your
name, e-mail address, telephone number, a unique User ID and password.
We also require that you provide the answers
to secret questions to be used when resetting your password.
Subscriber information: We generally obtain your name, address, telephone number, credit card number or other billing
information in order to process your subscription request.
As a subscriber, we request
that you also provide us with your e-mail address.
User accounts: You may review and update your account information and access your
transactions history.
==========
Sweepstakes, Contests, Surveys, Special Offers: On occasion, The New York Times may collect
additional personal information from you in connection with optional
sweepstakes, contests, surveys or special offers.
If this information is to
be shared with a third party, we will notify you at the time of
collection.
Online Reader Panel: Subscribers who agree to receive periodic
e-mails, and to participate in our online reader panels may be asked to answer
questions about themselves and our products.
This data is being collected for market research only, and no personally
identifiable information will be used or disclosed.
Times Talks, Arts & Leisure Weekends, other NY Times
Special Events and Promotions: We often receive information regarding
subscribers who may also be customers of Times Talks, Arts & Leisure
Weekends and other NY Times Special Events and Promotions.
Such information will be used as indicated
at the time of collection.
==========
Cookies: This
site employs cookies to recognize you and your access privileges on the site as
well as to track site usage.
IP Addresses:  This site logs IP addresses or the location of your computer on
the Internet for systems administration and troubleshooting purposes.
This information is logged on a separate
server from any personally identifiable information; our site's architecture does
not allow IP information to be linked to any personal information, such as a
user ID.
What does The New York Times do with the information it
collects?
==========
We respect your privacy.
The personal information you provide us will be used to help make our
future marketing efforts more efficient and to bring you offers of
interest.
If at any time you wish to
discontinue receiving any e-mails or offers, you may opt-out by following the directions in the Opt Out
section below.
We reserve the right
to contact you to discuss your account or to renew your subscription, in
accordance with applicable laws.
We also reserve the
right to disclose your opt-out information to third parties so that they can
"suppress" your name from future solicitations, in accordance with applicable
laws.
==========
With whom does The New York Times share the information
it gathers?
As a general rule,
we share the personal information we gather in aggregate form only with
advertisers and other partners.
Credit
card, billing and financial information is never disclosed, except as needed to
process the transaction, under strict confidentiality requirements.
From time to time, we provide trusted and
responsible third parties with customer information, provided that such parties
agree to use the information on a limited basis, and strictly in accordance
with our standard usage and non-disclosure agreement.
==========
Statistical, Demographic and Marketing Analyses.
The New York Times may perform statistical,
demographic and marketing analyses of subscribers and their subscribing and
purchasing patterns for product development purposes and to generally inform
advertisers about the nature of our subscriber base.
We also use such information to allow advertising to be targeted,
in aggregate, to the users for whom such advertising is the most
pertinent.
Personal information about
you as an individual will not be disclosed without your consent.
==========
E-mail and other communications.
We will periodically send you e-mails and
other communications regarding your account.
Products and services from The New York Times.
From time to time, we may provide you with
information and offers about other products and services offered by The New
York Times, including subscriptions, online archives, New York Times on the Web
e-mails, and other premium products and services.
==========
This information will only be shared internally.
If you do not wish to receive such
information or offers, you may opt-out.
Products and services from Third Parties or Partners.
From time to time, we may offer you the
opportunity to receive information and offers about products or services from
our partners or third parties.
If you
do not wish to receive such information or offers, you may opt-out.
How do I change or update my personal information?
You may update and/or edit
your account online on this site by clicking on the link for Update Account
.
==========
Should you require assistance, please
call our toll free number 1-800-NYTIMES.

If, at any time you wish to discontinue receiving any

campaigns originated by The New York Times Home Delivery Department, and its

mail and/or telephone solicitations, please notify us via email at:

==========
Compliance with Legal Process
We may disclose personal information if we are required to
do so by law or if we, in good faith, believe that such action is necessary to
(1) comply with the law or with legal process; (2) protect and defend our
rights and property; (3) protect against misuse or unauthorized use of this
site, or of The New York Times; or to (4) protect the personal safety or
property of our users or the public.
(For example, this means that if you provide false information or
attempt to pose as someone else, information about you may be disclosed.)
Data Security
To prevent unauthorized access, maintain data security, and
ensure the appropriate use of information, we have put in place appropriate
physical, electronic, and managerial procedures to protect the information we
collect online and offline.
Note: Even if you
have opted out under this policy, you may be contacted via e-mail or other
means with offers from The New York Times on the Web, which is a different
division of The New York Times Company.
Please visit www.nytimes.com
in order to update your email preferences for The New
York Times on the Web.
