Does Activision use cookies, track IP Addresses or use other passive means to collect information?
Activision uses cookies on its Web site.
A "cookie" is a small bit of record-keeping information that Web sites often store on a user�s computer.
Cookies do not include people�s names, e-mail addresses, or other information that is personally identifiable; they are used only to quickly identify your computer and to "remember" things about your visit, which will allow us to speed up your activities and save time you might otherwise normally have to spend during your visit.
You can disable cookies or set your browser to alert you when cookies are being sent to your computer.
We also keep track of Internet Protocol (IP) addresses.
An IP address is a number that is used by computers on the network to identify your computer every time you log on to the Internet.
