Collection and Use
New York Post (the "Post") recognizes the privacy concerns of visitors to our site on the Internet (www.nypostonline.com) and the importance of protecting the privacy of personally identifiable information collected from them.
As a general policy, no personally identifiable information, such as a visitor’s name, address, e-mail address, or telephone number, is automatically collected on our website.
Such information is collected only when knowingly and voluntarily submitted, and the Post uses such information only for the purpose for which it was submitted unless we disclose other uses in this Policy or at the time of collection.
We maintain reasonable and appropriate safeguards to ensure the security, integrity and privacy of such information in our possession.
==========
The Post does not sell personally identifiable information to third parties.
Cookies
Cookies are small bits of information that our site places on the hard drive of your computer to enable us to do a number of things to make your visit and future visits more enjoyable.
For example, cookies allow a visitor quickly to log on to a site he/she has used before by storing a password.
Cookies are not used to obtain information about a user that was not previously provided in a cookie.
The cookie itself does not contain any personally identifiable information about a user, and the Post does not use cookies in order to obtain such information.
Cookies are used by most major sites on the Internet.
Visitors may receive cookies from third parties, but the Post cannot control the content of these cookies.
Users should consult their browser owner’s manual for instructions on how to refuse cookies.
==========
IP Addresses
The Post may collect and disclose non-personal user information (for example, IP addresses) in aggregate form.
An IP address is a number given to a user by a Web Server.
Examples of information provided by an IP address include service provider, type of browser and type of system used.
The Post may use this information to make its site better and more responsive to the needs of the user.
This non-personal information may also be shared with third parties for marketing, advertising or research purposes.
An IP address is not used to obtain personal information about a user.
Obtaining At Home Delivery of the Post
Visitors to our site who want to receive at-home delivery of the New York Post can submit personal information to the site that makes possible a follow-up contact by a circulation representative.
The information is used only for this purpose.
==========
On-Line Surveys
The Post may conduct on-line marketing surveys that ask participants for demographic and other similar data (such as zip code, age, income and education level).
This information is used to make the Post more responsive to our readers and may be shared in aggregate, non-personal form with our advertisers and business partners.
On-Line Shopping
This site collects personally identifiable information in order to fulfill the purchases our visitors make through our on line shopping services.
The Post does not sell this information to third parties, or transfer it outside the company, except to our order fulfillment business partners.
In addition to using personally identifiable information to respond to visitors’ specific purchase orders and requests, the Post may use it for marketing, advertising or research purposes.
We may also use the information, or make it available to others, in non-personal, aggregate form to make our site better and more responsive to visitors’ needs or for marketing, advertising or research purposes.
Contests, Sweepstakes and Promotions
The Post may run contests, sweepstakes or promotions on our site that may require submission of personally identifiable information by participants.
Some of this information may be shared with a reputable outside company that administers the contest for the Post.
The Post may use the information collected during a contest in the aggregate so we can tell, for example, how many visitors to our site participate in the contest feature.
Comments and Submissions
The Post may obtain information from a user when he or she sends us comments, requests information on-line about the opportunities for advertising in the New York Post or responds to employment opportunities posted on our site.
The Post uses this information only for the purpose for which it is submitted, and does not share this information with third parties.
Any comments or ideas posted to this site or submitted to the Post become the property of the Post, which may use such comments or ideas in any way it sees fit.
