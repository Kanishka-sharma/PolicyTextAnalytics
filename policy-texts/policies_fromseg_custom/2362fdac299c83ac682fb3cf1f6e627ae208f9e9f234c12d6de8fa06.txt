Opera’s built-in Web search

This gives the user the option to utilize external Web search engines directly from the browser interface.
Opera relies on third parties for this service.

Opera’s geolocation support
Opera browsers with support for geolocation enable users to share their geographic location with websites.
If the user chooses to share their location with a web service, they have to enable geolocation support.
The location service is provided by Google and is governed by Google’s privacy policy.
The requesting websites’ use of your location is governed by the respective websites’ privacy policies.
Opera encourages users only to share their location with websites they trust.
If you use those services, Google receives information about your actual location (such as GPS signals sent by a mobile device) or information that can be used to approximate a location (such as a cell ID).
Opera does not receive or store any location information.
Google returns your location (longitude, latitude and accuracy) and your location is made available to the website by the Opera browser through the W3C Geolocation API.
