PRIVACY POLICY
INTRODUCTION
Welcome to Genius Sport's privacy policy.
Genius Sports Limited and Betgenius Limited (“we” or “our” or “us”) want to make sure all the personal information we have collected about you is safe and secure whether we collect it from you via electronic means (such as email or social media platforms), when you visit one of our global offices or in-person at a conference.
This notice sets out our commitments to you, in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (commonly known as the “GDPR”) and explains how we collect, store and use your personal information.
==========
Third-party links.
Our websites (www.geniussports.com, www.betgenius.com, www.geniussportsmedia.com) may include links to third-party websites, plug-ins and applications – for example, third party links to news stories about us.
==========
Clicking on those links or enabling those connections may allow third parties to collect or share data about you.
We do not control these third-party websites and are not responsible for their privacy statements.
When you leave our website, we encourage you to read the privacy policy of every website you visit.
COLLECTING YOUR INFORMATION
Website users
All of our pages on our website can be accessed without giving us your personal information (unless you click on a third party link (see Third-party links above)).
We only use essential cookies (see the Cookies section below).
==========
Direct Interactions
You may give us your name and contact information by filling in forms or by corresponding with us by post, phone, email, social media or otherwise.
This includes personal data you provide when you give us feedback or contact us.
Customers or prospective customers
If you are an individual that works for one of our customers (or prospective customers), you generally share with us (or we ask you for) your name, job title, address, email address and telephone number.
We may also keep records of your interactions with us, which may include an audio recording (where you have provided your consent for us to record).
Suppliers or prospective suppliers
If you are an individual that works at one of our suppliers (or prospective suppliers), we’ll ask you for your name, job title, email address and telephone number.
We may also keep records of your interactions with us, which may include an audio recording (where you have provided your consent for us to record).
Visitors
If you visit any of our offices, we’ll ask you for your name and signature and you may also choose to provide us with your email address and photograph.
We also have CCTV in operation in reception in every office.

WHO ELSE RECEIVES YOUR PERSONAL DATA?

TRANSFERRING YOUR DATA OUTSIDE THE EEA
By using our third party providers we may transfer the personal information we collect about you to the USA, if this is necessary, in order to perform our contract with you, or in accordance with any legitimate interests of Genius Sports.
Our third party providers including Workday Inc., Salesforce, Slack Technologies and Atlassian Inc. are registered with the US Privacy Shield.
This means that those organisations within the USA have been deemed by the European Commission to provide an adequate level of protection for your personal information.
We may also transfer your personal data to our employees in Medellin (Colombia).
==========

This means that Colombia is not deemed to provide an adequate level of protection for your personal information.

==========
HOW LONG WILL WE KEEP YOUR PERSONAL DATA FOR?
We collect personal data from you for different reasons and so we might keep it for different lengths of time.
==========
For:
• Individuals making direct interactions, it will generally be a period of 2 years after we were last in contact with you.
• Individual contacts at suppliers, this will be for as long as we continue to have a relationship with that supplier and then for a period of 6 years afterwards.
• Individual employees for our customers, this will be for as long as we continue to have a relationship with that customer and then for a period of 6 years afterwards.
• Visitors, it will generally be a period of 12 months after you last visited the office, except for CCTV footage which is automatically removed after 30 days (unless we need to retain the footage for investigation purposes).
==========

Please note that there are circumstances where your rights (as set out above) cannot be exercised for regulatory purposes.
==========

==========
Betgenius collects only Device-Identified Information (“DII”) where our Clients deploy the Betgenius Technologies on the websites you visit.
When we refer to DII in this policy, we mean information that is linked to a browser, device, group of devices, but is not used or intended to be used to directly identify you (or any particular individual).
We use DII browser session cookies and persistent cookies in our products and technology to provide, monitor, analyze, and improve our services.
==========
For more information about cookies please visit allaboutcookies.org.

We use the Betgenius Technologies to collect information.
The Betgenius Technologies consist of cookies, pixels, tags and other non-cookie technologies to collect and store data about devices, apps and web browsers (see “What information do we collect?”
section for more information on the types of data we collect).
==========
The Betgenius Technologies are embedded into digital advertisements, or otherwise deployed onto websites, which you have viewed or visited.

OPTING OUT

You may visit https://ssl.connextra.com/optoutstatus to opt-out of data collection.

Opting out does not mean that you will no longer receive online advertising.
It does mean that Betgenius will no longer collect any data associated with your activities.
