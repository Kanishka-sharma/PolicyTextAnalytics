What are Cookies and What Are They Being Used For?
To offer you better service when visiting websites most sites use cookies: practical technologies that collect and use information, making websites easier to use.
For instance, cookies ensure that you can remain logged in on a website or that your preferences, such as location and language settings, will be remembered.
As well, when shopping online cookies maintain your digital shopping cart.
Moreover, thanks to cookies website owners can see how often their sites - and which pages - are viewed by visitors.
Furthermore, some cookies make it possible to show your interest for a certain product.
==========
In doing so sites and advertisers will know something about your preferences but they will never know who you are.
In this way they can show you more relevant advertising.
In most cases advertising is necessary to finance websites.
==========
Thanks to cookies banners are adapted to the potential interests of website visitors, without the need to actually know their interests or know who they are.
The use of cookies is safe.
No personal information, such as phone numbers or e-mail addresses, can be derived from cookies.
This is why cookies cannot be used for e-mail and telemarketing actions.
Cookies are stored on your computer and you can remove them at any time.
This is easy to do.
==========
Click on one of the icons below to go directly to your browser manual.
We help you to make your own decisions about advertisements that are presented to you.
==========

If you want to turn off cookies from other parties, you can do so for several at once at www.youronlinechoices.com.
Keep in mind that if you opt-out, you will still see as many ads.
They will only be of less relevance to you.

To record how many orders have been placed through the advertisement.

Other / Unforeseen Cookies
We also use several third party providers to be able to place advertisements.
These parties use cookies as well.
We use the following categories of third parties:
Ad-serving providers: these are providers that make it technically possibly to show you an advertisement.
==========
Bid-exchange providers: these are marketplaces where the supply of advertisers and the demand of publishers meet.
Supply-Side Platform providers: these are providers that enable publishers to sell their available advertising space.
==========
Due to the way in which Internet and websites work, it is possible that we do not always have insight into the cookies that are placed through our advertisements by third parties.
This is particularly the case when our advertisements contain embedded elements such as texts, documents, images or film clips that are stored by another party, but that are shown on, in or through the advertisements placed by us.
==========
Should you encounter cookies that fall into this category which we have not mentioned here above, please let us know.
Or contact the third party directly and ask which cookies they have placed, what the reason for this is, what the lifetime of the cookie is and in what way they have safeguarded your privacy.
