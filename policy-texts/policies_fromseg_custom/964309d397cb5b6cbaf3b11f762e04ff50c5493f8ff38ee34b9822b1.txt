What personal information is collected?
Blizzard Entertainment only collects personal information on a voluntary basis.
If an on-line visitor submits information to Blizzard Entertainment, it will usually take the form of either: (1) the purchase of goods or services through our on-line stores; (2) product registration or registration for on-line game participation; (3) player match-up services; (4) warranty registrations; (5) contest registrations; (6) a consumer complaint; and/or (7) surveys.
Personal information collected may include your name, home address, phone number, e-mail address, age, and/or birth date.
We do not require this information to gain access to our sites, however, you will not be able to access certain areas that require registration or receive materials such as newsletters unless such information is provided.
Visitors purchasing products or services through our on-line stores will be required to provide additional information such as a valid credit card number and expiration date.

This information is never linked to you or your household in any way.

What are cookies and how do you use them?
Cookies are a feature of your web browser that allows web sites to transfer bits of information to your computer for record-keeping purposes.
A cookie stored on your computer can be used to "remember" things like your password, or that you have already registered.
Blizzard Entertainment may use cookies on its websites.
This allows us to speed up your future activities, saving the time you would normally spend entering information such as your password or registration.
In addition, cookies may be used to uniquely identify the user or to retrieve user information for promotional or marketing purposes.
==========
What are Internet protocol addresses?
An Internet protocol ("IP") address is the unique number assigned to your server or Internet Service Provider ("ISP").
Blizzard Entertainment may track such IP addresses for system administration or to report aggregate information.
Blizzard may also use IP addresses to limit utilization of our servers from any one system in an effort to prevent our servers from being abused.
