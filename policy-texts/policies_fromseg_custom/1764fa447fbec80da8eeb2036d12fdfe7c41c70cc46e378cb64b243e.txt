Cookies and similar technology; “Do not track” signals
There is a technology called “cookies” which can be used to provide you with tailored information from a website.
A cookie is an element of data that frequently contains an anonymous unique identifier; the data is sent from our website to your web browser, which may then store it on your system for future use.
Cookies are usually account identifiers and user preferences that allow you to speed through the log-in process by automatically providing the data so that you do not have to type it out and tailors your online experience in general.
Some Texas Roadhouse pages may use cookies so that we may better serve you when you return to our website and offer you certain services.
Texas Roadhouse also tracks the domains from which our website is visited and uses that data to analyze for trends and statistics.
==========
You can set your web browser to notify you when you receive a cookie, giving you the option to accept or reject it, or to refuse all cookies.
You may not be able to conduct some transactions or use some services on our website if you disable your web browser from accepting cookies.
We do not currently respond to browser “Do Not Track” signals.
For more information about third party cookies and tracking technologies, including information about how to opt out of certain of these technologies, visit one or both of the following:

State-Specific Rights
Your California privacy rights
California has enacted laws providing additional rights to California consumers regarding the collection and use of their personal information, including:
The right to know about personal information collected, disclosed or sold
California consumers have the right to request that Texas Roadhouse disclose what personal information we have collected, used, disclosed or sold in the prior 12 months.
Please refer to the disclosures throughout this Policy regarding the categories of personal information Texas Roadhouse has collected or disclosed in the last 12 months.
California consumers also have the right, subject to certain exceptions, to submit a verifiable request to obtain disclosure of personal information collected, used, or disclosed, by Texas Roadhouse in the prior 12 months.
Right to request deletion of personal information
Subject to certain exceptions, California consumers have a right to submit a verifiable request for the deletion of their personal information collected or maintained by Texas Roadhouse.
Once we receive and confirm your verifiable consumer request, we will delete (and direct our service providers to delete) your personal information from our records, unless an exception applies.
Right to non-discrimination for the exercise of consumer’s privacy rights
Consumers have the right not to receive discriminatory treatment by Texas Roadhouse for exercising any of the above California privacy rights.
Right to access and portability
Consumers have the right to receive certain information about our collection and use of their personal information over the past 12 months by submitting a verifiable request.
Once we receive and confirm your verifiable consumer request, we may disclose to you:
The categories of personal information we collected about you
The categories of sources for the personal information we collected about you
Our business or commercial purpose for collecting or selling that personal information
The categories of third parties with whom we share that personal information
The specific pieces of personal information we collected about you (also called a data portability request)
If we sold or disclosed your personal information for a business purpose, two separate lists disclosing:
For sales, the personal information categories that each category of recipient purchased; and
For disclosures for a business purpose, the personal information categories that each category of recipient obtained.

California verifiable requests
Who may submit requests?
Only you, or someone legally authorized to act on your behalf, may make a verifiable consumer request related to your personal information.
You may also make a verifiable consumer request on behalf of your minor child.
You may designate an authorized agent to make a request on your behalf by registering such person or entity with the California Secretary of State.
==========
How often can you submit a verifiable request?
You may only make a verifiable consumer request for access or data portability twice within a 12-month period.
How to submit a verifiable request?
To exercise the access, data portability, and deletion rights described above, please submit a verifiable consumer request to us by either calling us at 844-445-6675 or emailing us at privacy@texasroadhouse.com (opens a new tab/window) .
==========
Provide your full name, phone number, email address, state of residence and the type of request you are making.
We must verify your identity and authority to make a request before we provide you with personal information.
For certain requests, Texas Roadhouse will notify you if we need additional information to verify your request.
We will send you an email acknowledging that we received your request.
You must reply to the email and affirm that you made the request.
==========
We will use the data provided by the consumer to process the request.
We will respond within 45 days of your initial request.
If necessary, Texas Roadhouse may take an additional 45 days to respond to your request.
How Texas Roadhouse verifies and responds to requests
Your request must be verifiable.
==========
That means that you, or your authorized representative, must provide sufficient information that allows us to reasonably verify that you are the person about whom we collected personal information or an authorized representative of that person.
For example, if you are requesting access to or information about specific pieces of information, then we will require you to submit a signed declaration under penalty of perjury.
Scope of personal information provided in response to a request
Any disclosures we provide will only cover the 12-month period preceding our receipt of a verifiable request.
