Information that is Automatically Collected.
USATODAY.com also collects and stores information that is generated automatically as you navigate through the USATODAY.com website.
For example, to enhance your experience on the USATODAY.com website, USATODAY.com uses a standard feature found in browser software called a "cookie."
Cookies are small files that your web browser places on your hard drive for record-keeping purposes.
==========
By showing how and when visitors use the USATODAY.com site, cookies help us track user trends and patterns.
They also prevent you from having to re-enter your preferences on certain areas of the USATODAY.com site where you have entered preference information before.
==========
USATODAY.com also uses web beacons (also known as “single-pixel GIFs”) to access cookies and to count users who visit the USATODAY.com site or open USATODAY.com’s HTML-formatted email messages.
Another example of information that USATODAY.com automatically collects as you navigate the USATODAY.com site is information about your computer's connection to the Internet.
This information allows us, among other things, to improve the delivery of our web pages to you and to measure traffic on the USATODAY.com site.
In addition, third party advertisers or third party ad servers that place and present advertising on the Service also may collect information from you via cookies, web beacons, clear .
gifs or similar technologies.

These third party advertisers and ad servers may use the information they collect to help present their advertisements, to help measure and research the advertisements' effectiveness, or for other uses.
The use and collection of your information by these third party advertisers and ad servers is governed by the relevant third party’s privacy policy and is not covered by our privacy policy.
Indeed, the privacy policy of these third party advertisers and ad servers may be different from our privacy policy.
