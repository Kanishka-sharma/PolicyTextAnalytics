DEFINITIONS.
The terms listed below have the following meanings when used in this Policy:
 
- Aggregated Information is information about groups or segments of individuals, e.g., how many people accessed a Property on a given day or from a given geographical area.
Aggregated Information does not include Personally Identifiable Information.
- Anonymous Information is information that does not (and cannot reasonably be used to) identify an individual.
However, Anonymous Information when combined with Personally Identifiable Information becomes Personally Identifiable Information.
==========
- Client refers to an advertiser, agency, or buy-side platform that uses our Services pursuant to a written agreement with us.
- Cookies are files that Web servers place on an Internet user’s computer that are designed to store basic information and to recognize the user’s browser or device.
As discussed below, we use Cookies in connection with your use of the Site.
These Cookies do not store any Personally Identifiable Information.
==========
As with all Cookies, you can set your browser or device to notify you before you receive these Cookies so that you have the chance to accept them.
You can also set your browser or device so that it will not accept Cookies.
However, if you disable Cookies, then you will not have access to many features that make your interaction with our Site and the Services more efficient.
==========
- Interest Based Advertising uses information gathered across multiple Properties a user visits or from other sources in order to predict the user's preferences and to display advertising that is likely to be of most interest to the user.
- Personally Identifiable Information is information that can be used on its own or with other information to identify, contact, or locate a single person, or to identify an individual in context.
==========
- Web Beacon (or a “clear GIFs” or “pixel tag”) is software that can be used to display an image (usually a single pixel) on a website, email, or other communication.
A Web Beacon is usually used to transfer an individual’s unique user identification (often in the form of a Cookie) to a database in order to associate that individual with previously acquired information about that individual.
We use Web Beacons keep track of your online behavior in order to help us to determine your interests.
