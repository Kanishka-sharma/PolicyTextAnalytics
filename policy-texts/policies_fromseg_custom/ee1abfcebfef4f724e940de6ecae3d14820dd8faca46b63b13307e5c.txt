User conduct and fair play
Users, both registered and unregistered, agree to behave with good conduct whilst benefitting from Lichess' services.
Certain behavior will result in action being taken against the account including banning or closing the account.
Cheating is defined as using anything other than your own knowledge to play against another user, and gives you an advantage.
==========
This includes using computer engines on live games, using written chess materials on a live game such as opening books or endgame tablebases.
Having another player give you advice on what to play will also be considered cheating.
Exceptions are where the user is playing against the Stockfish engine, or against another player in a casual game with the opponent's consent.
